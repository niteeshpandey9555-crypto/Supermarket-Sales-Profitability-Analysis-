##  Overall Performance
- The supermarket generated strong overall revenue during the 3-month period, with March recording the highest sales.
- Gross income trends generally followed sales patterns, indicating stable pricing and margin structures across months.

---

##  Product Line Performance
- Health and Beauty emerged as the most profitable product category in terms of gross income.
- Some product lines generated high sales volumes but operated with below-average margins, indicating potential over-discounting or cost pressure.
- A small number of product categories contributed a disproportionately large share of total revenue, highlighting a clear Pareto (80/20) effect.

---

##  Customer Segmentation Insights
- Member customers contributed the majority of total revenue, demonstrating the effectiveness of the membership program.
- Members showed higher average bill values compared to normal customers, indicating stronger repeat purchasing behavior.
- Certain product lines performed significantly better among members than non-members.

---

##  Branch & City Performance
- Not all branches performed equally in terms of profitability.
- One branch underperformed on margin despite maintaining reasonable sales volume, suggesting operational inefficiencies or pricing issues.
- High-performing branches combined both strong revenue and healthy margins, making them benchmarks for best practices.

---

##  Payment Method Behavior
- Cash payments were associated with higher average basket values compared to card and e-wallet transactions.
- Digital payment methods showed higher transaction counts but lower average sales per transaction.
- Payment behavior varied by customer type, with members showing a stronger preference for digital payments.

---

## Customer Satisfaction vs Sales
- Higher customer ratings did not show a strong correlation with higher sales values.
- Product lines with excellent ratings did not always generate higher revenue, indicating that satisfaction alone does not directly drive spending.
- Sales performance appears to be more influenced by pricing, product mix, and customer segment than by ratings alone.

---

## 
Data Limitations & Assumptions
- The analysis is based on a limited time period (approximately three months), which restricts long-term seasonal trend evaluation.
- Net profit analysis was limited due to the absence of detailed operating cost data.
- All analyses were conducted assuming a clean dataset with no missing or null values.
